PHIL STANTON - RRR, Short Mem, & Beds The piano is a Reid-Sohn upright, nothing unusual, just your standard Korean-made average piano, about 11 years old. Miking (micing?) it up was tricky, I had to do a lot of playing around before I was remotely happy. Half the problem was that I only had a vocal mic (even though it's a really good AKG one), which isn't great for recording a piano. I also used a pair of Hohner Special 20 harmonicas (one C and one G), an old classical guitar, and some strange african bongos that my mum gave to my brother many years ago. The recordings were done on a Yamaha 4-track recorder, and pushed through a Korg guitar effects unit for things like chorus, delay and reverb.
