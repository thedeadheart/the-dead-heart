Seiden) TRIBUTE LINER NOTES for Back on the Borderline and Sins of Omission. Recorded in my basement - Detroit (Clawson), Michigan, Midwestern USA Back On The Borderline - This is the first Oils tune I remember hearing... at a dorm party sometime around late '83. I got hit in the forehead with a Fosters OILCAN at that same party... and I still have the scar. When I first heard about this Tribute I knew I'd have to do a version. Started in February of '98, this song has been through 3 versions since... disk errors, crashes, corrupted files, it's a wonder I ever got it done. Redneck Wonderland had a huge influence on my approach to the final version. Arrangement: Synthesizer bass, guitar, guitar feedback, many FX boxes, fuzzy vocals (me), Yamaha drum machine, acoustic snare, and LOTS of loops & editing. I used Rob's "stick hit/whoop" from the original for the intro rhythm. Sins of Omission - I'm a big fan of musical contrasts - the rawness of Breath was a refreshing break from album after album of stunning production - RSITS, D&D, BSM, ESM. In the early-mid 80s I played guitar in a couple of bands (BOB / Tool & Die) that did a lot of feedback/dirge/wall-of-sound/industrial surf-type tunes. Sins (especially the live version on 20,000 WSRL video) really appeals to the primal-scream guitar player in me. I wanted to contrast Sins' grungy lurching feel with some electronica elements & see what happened. Recorded entirely the last week of 1999. Vocals are courtesy of an old band-mate, Stefan Keydel--bassist & lead vocalist from the above-mentioned groups. I wound up using my old guitar tuner for the harmonica effects. Arrangement: LOTS of midi synths, synthesizer bass, guitar, guitar feedback, FX stomp boxes, acoustic snare, vocals (Stefan Keydel), and LOTS of editing. Instruments: SONY VAIO PC with Cakewalk & other misc. music software, EVENT Electronics DARLA digital audio card, NUMARK DJ mixer, Radio Shack vocal microphone, AudioTechnica stereo microphone, Gibson SG guitar, 10 or 15 FX stomp boxes, Fender Amp, KORG MonoPoly Synthesizer, homemade e-bow thingy, Yamaha RX-8 drum machine, Ludwig snare drum, and a pair of Midnight Oil drumsticks (courtesy of the Mercury Lounge).
